About Me

Data Science and Math major at Northeastern University.

I am a sophomore at Northeastern University, double-majoring in Data Science and Mathematics. I chose these fields because I enjoy finding patterns in data and building the quantitative skills that support both research and real-world applications. My coursework so far has included programming, statistics, and linear algebra, and I look forward to diving deeper into machine learning and data visualization as I progress through the program.

Outside of academics, one of my biggest passions is fencing. I find that the sport combines physical discipline with quick thinking and strategy. Whether I am practicing at the club or competing, fencing gives me a way to stay active and focused. It has also taught me the value of persistence and how to stay calm under pressure. I have written more about my fencing hobby on the next page.

At Northeastern, I am taking advantage of co-op opportunities and campus life to explore different career paths. I am interested in roles that sit at the intersection of data analysis and decision-making, and I hope to apply both my math background and my analytical skills in a future internship or research project. When I am not studying or fencing, I like to explore Boston and spend time with friends. I built this site to share a bit about myself and my interests—feel free to explore and get in touch.

My Hobby: Fencing

Fencing is a sport I have come to love for its blend of physical fitness, mental focus, and tactical thinking. It is one of the oldest Olympic sports and is often described as "physical chess" because every bout requires you to read your opponent, plan your actions, and react in split seconds. Whether I am at practice or at a competition, fencing pushes me to stay sharp both physically and mentally.

When you choose specialty coffee, you are supporting a more ethical and environmentally conscious industry. Many specialty roasters work directly with farmers, paying premiums that allow for better wages and sustainable farming practices. The attention to detail extends from the farm to your cup: green beans are carefully sorted, roasting profiles are developed to highlight each coffee’s unique characteristics, and baristas are trained to extract the best possible flavor. Whether you prefer a bright Ethiopian pour-over or a smooth Colombian espresso, the world of specialty coffee offers something for every palate.

Getting started at home is easier than you might think. Begin with freshly roasted beans—ideally within a few weeks of the roast date—and grind them just before brewing. Simple methods like the French press, AeroPress, or pour-over don’t require expensive equipment but can produce excellent results. The key is consistency: use a scale for your coffee and water, pay attention to water temperature, and take notes on what you enjoy. Over time, you’ll develop a deeper appreciation for the craft and the people behind every bag of beans.
